Projects

 

Preoperative and Intraoperative 3D Hologram Support with Mixed Reality Techniques in Liver Surgery

We developed an interactive 3D holographic imaging system as an intraoperative auxiliary tool, dedicated to promoting the development of precision surgery. The functions of the system include: liver anatomy and Couinaud segmented stereoscopic display, liver cross-section (resection margin) design and surgical 3D approach planning, preoperative "last minute" simulation, real-time calculation of intraoperative liver pre-cut volume, automatic registration of holographic liver with vivo liver (automatically registered to the patient's anatomy), holographic image sharing, and multi-person collaborative operation. This system will be integrated into clinical surgery.

[Project Page]

 

Keypoint-Driven Neural Head Avatars' Expression

In view of the huge volume of 3D face video data and the high complexity of traditional 3D video compression algorithms, a self-supervised learning-based 3D face video compression solution is being studied. The approach extracts face motion information by analyzing the three-dimensional structural features of the face and combines neural rendering to complete the neural head avatar coding and compression.

[Project Page]

 

A true 3D live broadcast system in Mixed Reality

The best way to foresee the future is to create it. The display technology of the future will not be restricted to a small mobile phone screen. We have developed a true 3D live broadcast system in mixed reality, aimed at bringing people an immersive chat experience. The whole system consists of raw point cloud data capture, preprocessing, compression, transmission, and rendering. Achieved a rendering rate of 15 frames per second on HoloLens2.

[Project Page]

 

Visual Switching Technology Based on Viewpoint Tracking

Accommodation-vergence conflict (ACV) is one of the main causes of visual fatigue in three-dimensional head-mounted displays (HMDs). The imaging technology of the current VR system cannot effectively alleviate the AVC effect. We used dynamic depth of field to reduce visual discomfort with HMDs based on the visual characteristics of the human eye, combined with light field imaging technology.
