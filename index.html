Projects

 

Real-Time Mixed Reality Guidance in Hepatectomy: Perform Simple, Clean, and Fast Surgery

Conventional hepatectomy faces many challenges. Specifically, the structure of liver blood vessels is complex, and intraoperative emergencies are common. Also, excessive liver resection will affect the postoperative liver function of patients, cause postoperative complications, and ultimately seriously hinder patients’ recovery. All these factors pose great challenges for surgeons to achieve a safe hepatectomy. In response to these challenges, we developed an interactive mixed reality operation-supportive system. This system provides real-time visualization of surgical anatomy to guide the implementation of resection plans. Animal experiments and clinical surgery have shown that doctors' abilities in surgical operations and intraoperative decision-making have both been improved with the help of mixed reality. The developed system provides a basis and a proof-of-concept for the application of mixed reality in future clinical surgery. Preoperative planning can be improved through quantifiable and controllable liver cross-section design. Visual guidance based on real-time and visualization, rather than surgeons' subjective judgment, can assist operators to formulate better intraoperative strategies, ultimately helping doctors perform the simplest, cleanest, and fastest surgery. Our system will be integrated into the standard operating procedures for hepatectomy. Our findings will promote the development of precision surgery.

 

Keypoint-Driven Neural Head Avatars' Expression

In view of the huge volume of 3D face video data and the high complexity of traditional 3D video compression algorithms, a self-supervised learning-based 3D face video compression solution is being studied. The approach extracts face motion information by analyzing the three-dimensional structural features of the face and combines neural rendering to complete the neural head avatar coding and compression.

A True 3D Live Broadcast System in Mixed Reality

The best way to foresee the future is to create it. The display technology of the future will not be restricted to a small mobile phone screen. We have developed a true 3D live broadcast system in mixed reality, aimed at bringing people an immersive chat experience. The whole system, which consists of raw point cloud data capture, preprocessing, compression, transmission, and rendering, achieved a rendering rate of 15 frames per second on HoloLens2.

Visual Switching Technology Based on Viewpoint Tracking

Accommodation-vergence conflict (ACV) is one of the main causes of visual fatigue in three-dimensional head-mounted displays (HMDs). The imaging technology of the current VR system cannot effectively alleviate the AVC effect. We used dynamic depth of field to reduce visual discomfort with HMDs based on the visual characteristics of the human eye, combined with light field refocusing technology.
